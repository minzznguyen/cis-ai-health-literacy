Environmental Health

Trusted resources for topics relating to environmental health.

  • Environmental Protection Agency (EPA): Environmental Topics
    The EPA provides data, research, and policy guidance on air quality, water safety, hazardous waste, and other key environmental health factors.
  • National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences (NIEHS)
    A branch of the NIH that focuses on research into how environmental exposures impact human health, including toxins, pollutants, and climate-related risks.
  • National Environmental Public Health Tracking Network (CDC)
    An interactive system from the CDC that tracks environmental hazards and health outcomes across U.S. communities to identify trends and inform public health action.
  • World Health Organization (WHO): Environment and Health
    WHO offers global research and data on environmental determinants of health, such as air pollution, water sanitation, and climate change.
  • United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP): Health and Environment
    UNEP provides international insights and reports on the intersection of environmental sustainability and human health, including pollution, biodiversity, and climate effects.
  • Earth Observatory (NASA): Health and Air Quality
    NASA’s Earth Observatory provides satellite-based data and research on global air quality, pollution patterns, and environmental health events like wildfires and dust storms.

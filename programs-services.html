Programs & Services

Trusted resources for topics relating to medical programs and services.

  • Medicaid & Medicare Services (CMS)
    Federal programs providing health coverage for eligible low-income individuals (Medicaid) and older adults or people with disabilities (Medicare). CMS also regulates healthcare quality standards and reimbursement policies.
  • Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA)
    A U.S. federal agency that provides healthcare to people who are geographically isolated, economically or medically vulnerable. Programs include health centers, maternal and child health, and rural health services.
  • SAMHSA (Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration)
    Provides support for mental health and substance use disorder treatment, including grants, educational tools, and a national helpline.
  • National Association of Free & Charitable Clinics (NAFC)
    Connects people with free or low-cost healthcare providers across the U.S., particularly for uninsured or underinsured patients.
  • Vaccines for Children Program (CDC)
    A federally funded program that provides free vaccines to children who might not otherwise be vaccinated because of inability to pay.
  • FindTreatment.gov
    A searchable directory managed by SAMHSA to locate nearby addiction and mental health treatment providers.
  • Women, Infants, and Children (WIC)
    A federal assistance program providing nutritious food, education, and healthcare referrals for low-income pregnant women, new mothers, and young children.
